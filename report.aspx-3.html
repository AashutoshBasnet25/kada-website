Philippines - Severe weather and floods, update (ECHO 14 Feb 2025)Wed, 12 Feb 2025 10:17

  • Heavy rainfall caused by the Shear Line continued to affect central and western Philippines, in particular the MIMAROPA region and the Eastern Visayas region since 6 February, causing floods and severe weather-related incidents that have resulted in an increasing number of population displacements and damage.
  • The Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD DROMIC) reports, as of 11 February, more than 12,638 evacuated people - of whom 10,187 in Mimaropa, and 219 in Eastern Visaya - across 72 evacuation centers, and 2,451 displaced people across both the affected regions. In addition, the same source reports a total of 133,934 affected people, of whom 48,638 in MIMAROPA and 85,296 across Eastern Visayas.
  • Over the next 48 hours, moderate rainfall is still forecast over central and western Philippines.
Philippines - Severe weather and floods, update (ECHO 14 Feb 2025)Thu, 13 Feb 2025 11:11

  • The heavy rainfall caused by the Shear Line continued to affect central and western Philippines, in particular the MIMAROPA region, the Eastern Visayas region, and the Bicol region over the last week, causing floods and severe weather-related incidents that have resulted in an increasing number of population displacements and damage.
  • The Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD DROMIC) reports, as of 12 February, nearly 11,000 evacuated people - of whom 10,317 in MIMAROPA, 289 in Eastern Visaya, and 338 in Bicol - across 128 evacuation centers, and around 4,700 displaced people across the three affected regions. In addition, the same source reports a total of approximately 164,800 affected people, of whom 63,440 in MIMAROPA, 94,919 across Eastern Visayas, and 6,437 in Bicol.
  • Over the next 96 hours, more rainfall is still forecast over central and western Philippines.
